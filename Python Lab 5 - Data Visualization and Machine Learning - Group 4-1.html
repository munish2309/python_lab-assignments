Exercise #1: Data Visualization¶

In [11]:
plt.figure(figsize = (20,10))
plot = sns.scatterplot(x='Carat Weight', y='Price', hue="Cut", style="Clarity", data = diamonds_df)
plot = plot.set(ylabel='Price in $', xlabel='Carat Weight')

Based on the graph it can be seen that nearly for every diamond, with the increase in carat weight, the price increases. However, a gap can be seen in between approx 1.9 - 2.0. As the carat weight increases after 2.0, the price for 'Ideal' cut increases drastically. Also the price of 'Signature-Ideal' is always higher as compared to other cuts for same carat weight. Whereas 'Very Good' cut lies a little lower to 'Ideal' cut prices for same carat weight. The price of 'Good' cut is the lowest among all other cuts.¶

Also, we can find that clarity of 'IF' and 'FL' usually have higher price.¶
